We explore the role of entropy in prediction and learning problems

# Example -- Classify Images

For every image $x$, our Neural Network $\text{NN}$ outputs a probability distribution over all possible labels $\mathscr l$
$$
Q_{\text{NN}}(\mathscr l\mid x)
$$
For every image $x$, the true label distribution is 
$$
P_{\text{true}}(\mathscr l\mid x)=\begin{cases}
1, & \mathscr l \text{ is the correct label for }x \\
0, & \text{wrong label}
\end{cases}
$$

Ideally, we want (for every pair image-label)
$$
Q_{\text{NN}}(\mathscr l\mid x)=P_{\text{true}}(\mathscr l\mid x)
$$
-> This will never happen !

Instead, we consider **cross entropy loss**

Concretely, for every image $x$, we wish to **minimize** the loss
$$
L(P_{\text{true}}(\cdot\mid x),Q_{\text{NN}}(\cdot\mid x))=-\sum_{\mathscr l = 1}^{K}P_{\text{true}}(\mathscr l\mid x)\log_{D}Q_{\text{NN}}(\mathscr l\mid x)
$$


>[!Definition]
>**Cross Entropy Loss** between two distributions $P$ and $Q$
>$$
>L(P,Q) = -\sum_{y}P(y)\log_{D}Q(y)
>$$

>[!Theorem]
>For a fixed probability distribution $P$, the minimum
>$$
>\min_Q L(P, Q)
>$$
>Is attained iff we select $Q^{*}=P$, and in this case would be given by
>$$
>L(P,Q^{*}) = L(P,P) = H(P)
>$$
