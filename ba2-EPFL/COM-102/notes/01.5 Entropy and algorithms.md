# The "20 Questions Problem"

Let $X$ be a random variable. 
- What is the minimum amount of boolean questions needed to identify $X$?
- Which questions should be asked

+ Consider a binary code $\Gamma$ for $X\in \mathcal X$
+ Once $\Gamma$ is fixed, we know $x\in \mathcal X$ <=> we know the codeword $\Gamma(x)$
+ We ask the $i$th question in order to obtain the $i$th bit
+ The average number of questions is $L(X,\Gamma)$, which is minimized when $\Gamma$ is a Huffman code

-> The sequence of Yes/No answers is a binary codeword associated to $x$

# Entropy and Sorting

Sorting with pairwise comparisons of two elements, with binary output -> Either $<$ or $\geq$ 

But how many binary comparisons are needed ?

$$
\mathbb E[\text{number of comparisons}] \geq H_{2}(X)
$$

$$
N\geq\max H_{D}(X) = \log_{3}|\mathcal X|
$$
# Billiard Balls

We have 14 balls, and we have a ball as a reference (we know it's normal).
At most one ball can be heavier/lighter than the others. 

> How can find which ball is different ?

We can identify the solution with a random variable $X$
$$
\begin{array}l
\text{all are heavy} & X=0 \\
\text{Ball 1 heavy} & X=1 \\
\text{Ball 1 light} & X=-1 \\
\text{Ball 2 heavy} & X=2 \\
\text{Ball 2 light} & X=-2 \\
\vdots & \vdots \\
\text{Ball 13 heavy} & X=13 \\
\text{Ball 13 light} & X=-13
\end{array}
$$

We have $X\in\mathcal X=\{ -13,-12,\dots, -1, 0, 1, \dots, 12, 13 \}$
Then the we have the size of the alphabet $|\mathcal X| = 27$
Therefore the amount of ternary symbols needed to represent the source is at least
$$
N\geq H_{D=3}(X)
$$
Which we have the the amount of comparisons as
$$
\max H_{D=3}(X)=\log_{3}|X|=\log_{3}27=3
$$
Hence we need to do at least 3 comparisons (weighings)
