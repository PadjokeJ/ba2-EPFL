In practice, the outputs of sequential logic elements connects to the inputs of other sequential elements.

For correct operations, timing constraints must be met.

A signal on input $D$ of a DFF must be stable during (before and after) the **rising edge** of the clock (so that the correct data gets read)


> [!Definition] Metastability
> If a signal is *not* stable around the rising clock edge, then a DFF will oscillate at a value between $0$ and $1$, before settling on a random value. 

For a circuit to meet those timing constraints, then all FF outputs to FF inputs must meet timing constraints. In order to do so, we:
- Must have a stable signal for $t_{\text{setup}}$ before and $t_{\text{hold}}$ after the rising clock edge

A signal goes through a few steps:  

![[Pasted image 20260413133545.png]]

## Born as $Q$ of the source FF

There can be a delay $t_{cQ}$ between $t_{cQ,\min}$ and $t_{cQ,\max}$

Updating the output takes some clock-to-$Q$ delay
## Journey through combinational logic

It takes time of $t_{\text{comb}}$ for the signal to be processed during the combinational logic. 

During this time the signal outputted can be unstable
## Arrives at the Destination FF

After a total time of $t_{cQ}+t_{\text{comb}}$, the signal arrives at the destination where it will be captured

## Waits to be captured

It waits -- in a stable manner -- at the input of the next flip flop. This wait must at least be as long as the setup time $t_{\text{setup}}$

## Gets captured on the clock edge

The destination takes the value on its input, and works through memorizing it. (Which can take some time)

## Waits to be replaced

The clock edge that captured is passed. The wait must be at least as long as the hold time $t_{\text{hold}}$ (And it must be stable!!).

# Meeting time constraints

To setup the signal, we have the following:

For $t_{cQ}$ and $t_{\text{comb}}$, we must have the inequality given by
$$
T_{\text{CLK}}- (t_{cQ} + t_{\text{comb}}) \geq t_{\text{setup}}
$$
The worst case time constraint can be rewritten as
$$
\boxed{t_{cQ,\max} + t_{\text{comb}, \max} + t_{\text{setup}} \leq T_{\text{CLK}} = 1 / f_{\text{CLK}}}
$$
We have an algorithm to find this maximum frequency $f_{\max}$

> [!Algorithm] 
> 1. Identify all $Q$-to-$D$ paths
> 2. For every path
> 	a. Compute its longest combinational delay
> 	b. Find the shortest clock period that satisfies this path's setup-time constraint
> 3. Find the shortest clock period $T_{\text{CLK}}$ that satisfies the setup-time constraints of all those paths
> 4. Compute $f_{\max} = 1 / T_{\text{CLK}}$

To hold the signal, we have the following:

For $t_{cQ}$ and $t_{\text{comb}}$, we have:
$$
t_{cQ}+t_{\text{comb}}\geq t_{\text{hold}}
$$
The worst-case constraint is written as 
$$
\boxed{t_{cQ,\min} +t_{\text{comb},\min} \geq t_{\text{hold}} }
$$
To find hold-time violations, we have

> [!Algorithm]
> 1. Identify all $Q$-to-$D$ paths
> 2. For every such path
> 	a. Compute its shortest combinational delay
> 	b. Verify if the hold-time constraint is satisfied

# Clock Skew

In real circuits, the clock signal may not arrive at all FFs at the same time, due to the wires

We call this delay **clock skew** -- which is given by
$$
t_{\text{skew}} = \Delta_{D} - \Delta_{S}
$$

![[Pasted image 20260413142035.png]]

This should not be neglected!!

We have both a *negative* and a *positive* clock skew
- Negative: $t_{\text{skew}} < 0$
- Positive: $t_{\text{skew}} > 0$

With clock skew, we see [[#Meeting time constraints|life of the D signal]] change slightly, as we need to account for the possible delays in clocks for the **hold** and the **setup** time

The expression that must hold has the skew added in:
$$
T_{\text{CLK}} + \Delta_{D}-(\Delta_{S} + t_{cQ}+t_{\text{comb}})\geq t_{\text{setup}}
$$
And we have the worst-case scenario as
$$
t_{cQ,\max} + t_{\text{comb},\max} + t_{\text{setup}} - (\Delta_{D} - \Delta_{S})\leq T_{\text{CLK}} = 1 / f_{\text{CLK}}
$$

And to meet hold time constraints, we have
$$
t_{cQ}+t_{\text{comb}}- (\Delta_{D} - \Delta_{S})\geq t_{\text{hold}}
$$
And the worst-case scenario as
$$
t_{cQ,\min} + t_{\text{comb}, \min} - (\Delta_{D} - \Delta_{S}) \geq t_{\text{hold}}
$$
> [!Tip] Positive time skew **INCREASES** max frequency
