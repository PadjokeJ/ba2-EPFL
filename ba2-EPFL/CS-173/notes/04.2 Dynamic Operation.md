*Fan-in* is the number of a logic gate's inputs

*Fan-out* is the number of gate inputs connected to this logic gate

Parasitic capacitance is the unavoidable plague of electronic circuits. It makes circuits affect the other close circuits in an unwanted manner, which causes them to behave differently. The sum of all the capacitances gives the capacitance of that logic gate.

A capacitor takes time to charge and discharge -> Kirchhoff's law
- Charging : $V_{c}(t) = V_{DD}e^{ - \frac{t}{RC} }$
- Discharging : $V_{c}(t) = V_{DD} (1-e^{ - \frac{t}{RC} })$

The *speed*, or delay, depends on how fast the load capacitance is charged
- With higher fan-in the per-input capacitance is higher
- With higher fan out, the higher total load capacitance

*The higher the capacitance, the slower the gate is*
